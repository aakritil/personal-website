About

A Study on the Coagulating Properties of the M. oleifera Seed

Whenever I visited India, I saw how many communities around me were struggling to access clean water. At the same time, there were many natural resources nearby that could be utilized to help fix this problem. In my backyard, I grow a plant known as M. Oleifiera, a plant native to India that grows along streets. While researching fertilizer methods for my plants, I learned that the seeds of the plant contain a protein that could be used for coagulating purposes within the process of water filtration. This discovery sparked a research project using the protein in combination with functionalized sand to reduce the fecal bacterial load in water and produced promising results for the future of natural water filtration.

Characterization of LEDs and a Study of their Applications

Light emitting diodes, otherwise known as LEDs, are an upcoming form of lighting that have many different applications. Compared to other forms of lighting, LEDs are cheaper, last longer, and are more resilient in different areas. Some of these applications include LED grow lights, photodynamic therapy, and water sterilization. The goal of this project is to examine the Color Rendering Index (CRI) of lighting devices and their emerging applications, as well as examine how LED light effects mood. A set of LEDs with varying wavelengths, from 505 nanometers to 940 nanometers, were characterized through a series of experiments that analyzed the spectrum and found Plank’s Constant. A spectral light meter was used to analyze the spectrum, as well as analyze different units such as the Photosynthetically Active Radiation (PAR) and the actual CRI measurements. Overall, a greater understanding of LEDs and their interaction with the real world was gained.

First Mercury and Silver Complexes of a Bulky Heterocyclic Thione Ligand

The heterocyclic thione ligand Me2IiPrS was first synthesized by Kuhn and Kratz in 1993, and has been subsequently used to prepare complexes for a variety of main group and transition metals, including aluminum, zinc, copper, rhodium, manganese, iron, cobalt, chromium, and nickel. We describe in this presentation our initial attempts to synthesize the first examples of mercury complexes with this ligand. More specifically, two separate series of mercury(II) complexes have been isolated, namely (Me2IiPrS)nHgX2 (n = 1, 2; X = Cl, Br, I). A discussion of the characterization data for these compounds, including nuclear magnetic resonance(NMR) spectroscopy data and elemental analysis is presented.
