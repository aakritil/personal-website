Characterization of LEDs and a Study of their Applications

Light emitting diodes, otherwise known as LEDs, are an upcoming form of lighting that have many different applications. Compared to other forms of lighting, LEDs are cheaper, last longer, and are more resilient in different areas. Some of these applications include LED grow lights, photodynamic therapy, and water sterilization. The goal of this project is to examine the Color Rendering Index (CRI) of lighting devices and their emerging applications, as well as examine how LED light effects mood. A set of LEDs with varying wavelengths, from 505 nanometers to 940 nanometers, were characterized through a series of experiments that analyzed the spectrum and found Plankâ€™s Constant. A spectral light meter was used to analyze the spectrum, as well as analyze different units such as the Photosynthetically Active Radiation (PAR) and the actual CRI measurements. Overall, a greater understanding of LEDs and their interaction with the real world was gained.
